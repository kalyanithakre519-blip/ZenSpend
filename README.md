# ZenSpend: AI-Driven Spending Control Tool 🧘🧠

ZenSpend is a Chrome Extension designed to introduce **intentional friction** into the online shopping experience. It detects impulsive checkout attempts and encourages mindfulness by calculating the true cost of items in "Life Hours."

## ✨ Features

- **Platform Detection**: Automatically identifies "Buy Now" and "Add to Cart" actions on **Amazon**, **Walmart**, and **Shopify** stores.
- **Cooling-Off Period**: Injects a mandatory 60-second "Mindful Pause" before a purchase can be finalized.
- **Life-Hour Calculator**: Translates product costs into hours of manual labor based on your custom hourly wage.
- **AI-Driven Reflections**: Displays dynamic, context-aware mindfulness prompts based on the price of the item.
- **Premium Aesthetics**: Features a modern, dark-mode design with glassmorphism and smooth animations.

## 🚀 How to Install & Use

1. **Open Chrome Extensions**:
   - In your Chrome browser, go to `chrome://extensions/`.
2. **Enable Developer Mode**:
   - Toggle the "Developer mode" switch in the top-right corner.
3. **Load the Extension**:
   - Click the **"Load unpacked"** button.
   - Navigate to the folder: `c:\Users\ishwa\Ai-Driven Impulse Spending control tools`.
   - Select the folder to install.
4. **Configure Your Settings**:
   - Click the ZenSpend icon in your extensions toolbar.
   - Enter your **Hourly Wage** (e.g., $45).
   - Ensure **Intentional Friction** is toggled ON.
5. **Start Shopping Mindfully**:
   - Visit [Amazon](https://amazon.com) or any Shopify store.
   - Click a "Buy Now" or "Add to Cart" button.
   - Experience the **Mindful Pause** overlay!

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

- **Frontend**: Vanilla JavaScript, HTML5, CSS3 (Modern Flexbox/Grid).
- **APIs**: Chrome Extensions API (V3), Storage, Scripting.
- **Design**: Google Fonts (Outfit), Custom CSS Glassmorphism.

## 🧠 Why ZenSpend?

Impulsive spending is driven by speed and convenience. ZenSpend breaks this cycle by forcing a temporal gap between the *impulse* and the *action*, allowing your prefrontal cortex to regain control over your financial decisions.
